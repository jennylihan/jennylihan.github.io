I am an educator, designer, and researcher.


I'm currently pursuing my Master's in Computer Science, as part of the Stanford HCI research group. I care a lot about designing for equity and designing for learner empowerment. During my gap year, I was a computer science teacher and researcher in Hong Kong for the Transformative Learning Technologies Lab, designing and investigating a constructionist, student-driven, project-based curriculum. Most recently this year, I was the TA for CS377U (Understanding Users) and the course designer of the inaugural Snap Engineering Academy.





The short list of my education:

  • In 2019, I was awarded a Fulbright Research Scholarship to study at Peking University in China and conduct field work on technology use in rural elementary schools. The U.S.-China Fulbright program was indefinitely suspended in July of 2020.
  • As an undergrad at Stanford, I studied Symbolic Systems, an interdisciplinary major combining, philosophy, psychology and computer science.
  • For 3 months, I studied abroad in Santiago, Chile, and wrote a paper exploring how the language we speak affects the emotions we feel.
  • My teachers were my greatest role models growing up in Bay Area public schools, and I feel strongly about contributing to public education in the future.
