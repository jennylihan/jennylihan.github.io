Install Vitae

We're working on a way to make it easier for communities of specialized workers to do complex work for more money by sharing these groups between requesters, and making it possible for workers themselves to reject tasks that don't follow the rules that the community sets.

The system we're building is called "Vitae". It uses a Chrome extension to annotate your HIT list, and eventually it'll be able to aggregate your work history so Vitae can say that you're an expert at image tagging tasks, transcription tasks, tasks for academic institutions, etc...

We'd like you to install Vitae, give some feedback on getting started, and (hopefully) use it for a while as Vitae-enabled tasks show up on Amazon Mechanical Turk. Tasks that use Vitae will only be accessible to Turkers who have the Chrome extension installed.

Important: the chrome extension will use your worker ID, your total number of approved HITs, and your total number of rejected HITs from the Dashboard. If you are not okay with this, do not accept this HIT.

Go here to install Vitae.

After you've installed the extension, click on the extension. Go through the sign up process and tutorial until you hit the Dashboard. Then come back to give some feedback.

We will put you into your first community based on your answers to these questions:

1. On a scale from 1 (not confident) to 10 (very confident), how confident are you in the quality of your own work?

2. Explain in 100 characters or more why the quality of your own work is important to you.

3. What is the lowest amount in dollars that you would work per hour?

4. Is it important that requesters send high-quality or high-complexity tasks? Explain in 100 characters or more how you decide which tasks to do.
